About SenBulk

SenBulk is a visualization platform for pooled bulk RNA-seq analysis of senescent cells.

It helps researchers check whether a gene is consistently changing across different senescence inducers, cell lines, and experimental settings by bringing study-level results into one searchable view.

SenBulk is a distributable version of the pooled bulk analysis presented in our SenFlag paper (https://doi.org/10.1038/s44318-026-00845-6; see Fig. EV1B)

      How to read it

      • Rows are sorted from highest to lowest reported log2FC; senescent vs ctrl. conditions.
      • Horizontal bars show lfcSE from DESeq2.
      • When effect bars of one study cross the zero line on the Y-axis, the results are statistically non-significant for that study.
      • A 'comparison' refers to a single senescent vs. ctrl. test from a dataset.
      • A single study may have multiple comparisons because some studies include several senescence conditions and corresponding matched controls.
      • Absent comparisons suggest insufficient statistical confidence for the specified gene in the unavailable datasets.
      • Gene correlations are computed using Pearson coefficients on log2FC values from senescent vs. ctrl. conditions.
      • All analyses were conducted using DESeq2 with data obtained from ARCHS4.
